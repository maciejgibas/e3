About the department

A team connecting electronics, computing, and automation

The Department of Infotronics and Cybersecurity E-3 is a unit of the Faculty of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cracow University of Technology. We conduct research, teaching, and industry collaboration in infotronics, automation, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity.

Our research spans system identification, digital control, building automation, 3D printing, and advanced computing technologies.

Teaching

Infotronics — an interdisciplinary program

Infotronics combines mechatronics, electronics, computer science, and automation. Students learn to design, build, prototype, and operate advanced technical systems that integrate multiple technology domains.

Design of cyber-physical systems Process modeling and digital control Embedded systems and prototyping Data analysis and AI in engineering

Practical skills

Graduates gain skills needed in modern industry, R&D centers, and technical research. The program develops both technical expertise and the ability to work in interdisciplinary project teams.

Cybersecurity

Profile and impact

Cybersecurity is a key area of education and research, addressing current market needs. Students build competencies in network security, operating systems, web applications, and cloud technologies, as well as protection methods and vulnerability assessment.

Program history

Infotronics — context and significance

History of Infotronics at CUT

The Infotronics program at Cracow University of Technology emerged from the need for interdisciplinary engineering skills, combining electronics, mechanics, and computing in one curriculum. It is delivered within the Faculty of Electrical and Computer Engineering, preparing students for rapidly evolving technology sectors.

History and significance

The curriculum equips graduates with practical and theoretical knowledge required for advanced technological industries, fostering innovation and cross-domain collaboration.
