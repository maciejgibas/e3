About

Profile, mission, and areas of activity

The Department of Infotronics and Cybersecurity E-3 is a unit of the Faculty of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cracow University of Technology. We combine research, teaching, and industry collaboration in infotronics, automation, AI, and cybersecurity.

Interdisciplinary Applied research Industry collaboration
Overview

Modern engineering in one place

The department conducts interdisciplinary research and teaching, developing technologies in system identification, digital control, building automation, 3D printing, and advanced computing.

Our mission is to bridge theory with practical industrial implementations and prepare students for work in a dynamic technology environment.

Department mission

We build competencies at the intersection of hardware and software, delivering solutions for industry and modern cyber-physical systems.

Teaching

Infotronics program

Infotronics combines mechatronics, electronics, computer science, and automation. Students learn to design, build, prototype, and operate advanced technical systems that integrate multiple technological domains.

Graduate competencies

Skills valued in modern industry and technical research: data analysis, embedded systems design, digital control, and AI in engineering.

Cybersecurity

Profile and impact

Cybersecurity addresses market needs related to protection of ICT systems, applications, and data. Students gain competencies in network security, operating systems, web applications, and cloud technologies.

Networks and infrastructure Monitoring and protection
Systems and applications Vulnerability analysis
Cloud Data security
